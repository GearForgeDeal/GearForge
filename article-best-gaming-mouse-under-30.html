What We Looked For

We judged each mouse on five criteria:

  • Sensor accuracy — does it track without jitter or acceleration?
  • Build quality — does it feel solid or cheap?
  • Comfort — can you use it for 3+ hour sessions without fatigue?
  • Software — is there an app, and is it worth using?
  • Value for money — is the price justified?

The 5 Best Gaming Mice Under €30

🥇 1. Logitech G203 LIGHTSYNC — Best Overall

The G203 uses Logitech's own HERO sensor (8,000 DPI), which is genuinely excellent for this price. It tracks precisely with no hardware acceleration. The build is compact plastic but feels well-assembled. Six programmable buttons, full RGB via Logitech G HUB software, and an ambidextrous shape that fits most hand sizes.

It's been our top budget mouse pick for over a year and nothing has knocked it off the top spot yet.

The DeathAdder Essential is the budget sibling of Razer's legendary ergonomic mouse line. It uses a solid 6,400 DPI optical sensor (not as precise as the G203, but still very good), and the right-handed ergonomic shape is extremely comfortable for claw and palm grip users.

No RGB (just a Razer logo glow), but the scroll wheel and side buttons feel premium. Great for gamers who prioritize comfort over RGB bling.

Our Final Recommendation

If you want the absolute best sensor quality under €30, the Logitech G203 is the undisputed choice. It uses the same sensor philosophy as mice costing 3x more, and Logitech's software ecosystem is excellent.

If ergonomics are your priority (palm grip, long sessions), go with the Razer DeathAdder Essential. The G203 is symmetrical and slightly smaller — not ideal for all hand sizes.
