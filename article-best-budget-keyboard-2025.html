What Makes a "Good" Budget Keyboard?

At this price point, you're looking for:

  • Real mechanical switches — not membrane or "chiclet" style. Look for Outemu, Gateron, or Cherry MX switches.
  • Metal top plate — prevents flex and feels solid.
  • N-key rollover — all keys register simultaneously (important for gaming).
  • Per-key or zone RGB — looks good, useful in the dark.
  • Detachable cable — nice to have; protects against cable damage.
